# Security Policy

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Do not report security issues in public Discussions or issues.** Anything you'd rather not see on a billboard belongs in a private channel.

If you've found a security vulnerability in the MatchRox iOS app or its backend, please report it privately one of two ways:

1. **GitHub private security advisory** (preferred) — [Report a vulnerability](https://github.com/Matchroxx/community/security/advisories/new). GitHub walks you through the disclosure form and only the MatchRox team sees it.
2. **Email** — `security@matchrox.com`. PGP optional; plaintext is fine for initial contact.

## What to include

- A clear description of the issue and the potential impact
- Steps to reproduce
- App version and platform (e.g. iOS 26.1, MatchRox 1.4.2)
- Any proof-of-concept you have

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgment within 3 business days.** If you haven't heard back, assume the message got lost and email again.
- We'll triage and get back to you with a severity assessment and timeline.
- We'll credit you in release notes (if you want) once the fix ships.

## Scope

In scope:

- The MatchRox iOS app
- MatchRox backend APIs and services
- Anything at `*.matchrox.com`

Out of scope:

- Social engineering of MatchRox employees or athletes
- Physical attacks
- Denial of service
- Spam or content moderation issues (use the in-app report flow for those)

## Safe harbor

We won't pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Report vulnerabilities through the channels above
- Don't access or exfiltrate other users' data beyond what's needed to demonstrate the issue
- Don't intentionally degrade service for other users
- Give us a reasonable window to fix before any public disclosure
